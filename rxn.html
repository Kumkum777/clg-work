Types of chemical reactions

1. Based on Energy Change



a:- Exothermic Reaction Definition:- Energy is released to the surroundings.

Example:- 𝐶 𝐻 4 + 2 𝑂 2 → 𝐶 𝑂 2 + 2 𝐻 2 𝑂 + heat CH 4 ​ +2O 2 ​ →CO 2​ +2H 2 ​ O+heat



b:- Endothermic Reaction Definition:-Heat energy is absorbed from the surroundings.

Example:- 𝐶 𝑎 𝐶 𝑂 3 → heat 𝐶 𝑎 𝑂 + 𝐶 𝑂 2 CaCO 3 ​ heat ​ CaO+CO 2 ​



2. Based on How Atoms Rearrange



a:- Combination (Synthesis) Reaction Definition:

Two or more reactants combine to form a single product.

Example:2H2 + O 2 → 2H2O


b:- Decomposition Reaction Definition:- One compound breaks down into two or more simpler substances.

Example:- 2Hg O+ → 2Hg + O 2


C:- Displacement (Single Replacement) Reaction Definition:- A more reactive element displaces a less reactive element from a compound.

Example:-Zn + CuSO4→ ZnSO4 +Cu
